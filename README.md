# Maternal-HealthRisk-Analysis

📖 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing maternal health data to identify risk factors during pregnancy. Using Excel for data cleaning and preprocessing, and Power BI for interactive dashboard creation, the project highlights how clinical, physiological, and historical health information can be transformed into actionable insights.
The dataset includes variables such as:
• 	Age
• 	Blood Pressure (Systolic & Diastolic)
• 	Blood Sugar (mmol/L)
• 	Body Temperature (°F)
• 	Body Mass Index (kg/m²)
• 	Preexisting & Gestational Diabetes
• 	Mental Health Issues
• 	Heart Rate
• 	Risk Level (High, Low, Unknown)

📊 Dataset Source

The dataset used in this project is the Maternal Health Risk Assessment Dataset, published on Mendeley Data:
• 	Link: Maternal Health Risk Assessment Dataset
• 	Published: 1 November 2024  Version 1
• 	Description: This dataset comprises detailed clinical, physiological, and historical health information collected from maternal patients to evaluate potential health risks during pregnancy. It serves as a resource for developing predictive models aimed at identifying and managing high-risk pregnancies, providing insights into maternal health factors, and supporting personalized patient care. The dataset is well-suited for research in obstetrics, predictive health modeling, and maternal healthcare management.


📊 Data cleaning and preparation

Performed in Excel:
• 	Removed duplicates and corrected missing values
• 	Standardized headers with proper units (mmHg, mmol/L, °F, kg/m²)
• 	Applied conditional formatting to highlight high-risk indicators
• 	Created pivot tables and charts for exploratory analysis

📊 Dashboard Development

Built in Power BI:
• 	Interactive visuals to explore risk levels by age, BMI, blood sugar, and diabetes status
• 	Drill-down filters for gestational vs. preexisting diabetes
• 	Comparative charts showing averages of blood sugar, BMI, and heart rate across risk categories
• 	Highlighted correlations between mental health issues and high-risk pregnancies
🌟 Key Insights
• 	Blood Sugar: Elevated values (>9 mmol/L) strongly correlate with high-risk classification.
• 	Diabetes: Both preexisting and gestational diabetes significantly increase risk.
• 	Mental Health: Nearly 70% of high-risk patients also experience mental health issues.
• 	BMI Extremes: Very high or very low BMI values are linked to risk.
• 	Hypertension: Common among high-risk patients, but not all hypertensive cases are classified as high risk—showing multifactorial dependence.
• 	Age & Body Temperature: Limited impact compared to other variables.

🌍 Importance of Maternal Health Risk Analysis
Maternal health remains a global public health priority. Complications during pregnancy can lead to severe outcomes for both mother and child. By analyzing such datasets:
• 	Healthcare professionals can identify early warning signs.
• 	Policymakers can design targeted interventions for vulnerable groups.
• 	Families and communities can be more aware of mental health’s role in maternal well-being.
In today’s world, where maternal mortality and morbidity are still pressing issues, data-driven insights empower better decision-making and improve healthcare delivery.
